Understanding Rule-Based Expert Systems

Discover how classical diagnostic logic compares to contemporary machine learning frameworks.

Rule-Based Expert System

IF-ELSE Logic Paradigm

Expert systems utilize explicit clinical conditions written as strict programmatic logic. They are deterministic, highly transparent, easy to audit, and don't require training datasets. The logic follows a structured path modeled after human physician protocols (e.g. F.A.S.T.).

  • Decision Making: Structured boolean paths and weights.
  • Classification: Exact thresholds determine severe warnings.
  • Search Logic: Predictable, linear complexity mapping input fields.

Modern Machine Learning

Probabilistic Weights

Unlike expert systems, machine learning utilizes neural networks and statistical models that learn coefficients automatically from large amounts of historical patient records. They excel at recognizing hidden patterns in fuzzy data, but act as complex black-boxes that can be difficult to interpret mathematically.

  • Decision Making: Multi-dimensional layers of weights/biases.
  • Classification: High-dimensional boundaries fit on huge curves.
  • Search Logic: Complex mathematical gradient descent optimization.
